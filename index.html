The property manager then works to ensure that the owner's goals are met by managing rent, tenants, maintenance, budgets, and rental property records. They must also have an in-depth familiarity of state and national laws regarding the legal methods to screen tenants, handle security deposits, terminate leases, conduct evictions, and comply with property safety standards. For these reasons, some states require property managers to be licensed real estate brokers. If this is the case, a property owner will need to hire a broker to ensure their property is managed legally.

 

Other states allow for managers to be licensed in property management instead of as realtors, while some don't require licensing at all. In addition to licensing, property managers come with different specialties and experiences.
